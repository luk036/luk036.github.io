15  * @brief Partition Manager Base

17  * @tparam GainMgr
18  * @tparam ConstrMgr
19  * @tparam Derived
20  *
21  * Iterative Improvement Partitioning Base Class. In this
22  * partitioning method, the next solution $s'$ considered after
23  * solution $s$ is dervied by first applying a sequence of
24  * $t$ changes (moves) to $s$ (with $t$ dependent from
25  * $s$ and from the specific heuristic method), thus obtaining a
26  * sequence of solution $s,...,s_t$ and by successively
27  * choosing the best among these solutions.
28  *
29  * In order to do that, heuristics refer to a measure of the gain (and
30  * balance condition) associated to any sequence of changes performed on
31  * the current solution. Moreover, the length of the sequence generated
32  * is determined by evaluting a suitably defined $stopping rule$ at
33  * each iteration.
34  *
35  * Reference:
36  * G. Ausiello et al., Complexity and Approximation: Combinatorial
37  * Optimization Problems and Their Approximability Properties, Section 10.3.2.
38  */
