15 /**
16  * Monomial function object. Let $x_1$, ..., $x_n$ denote $n$ real
17  * positive variable and x = ($x_1$, ..., $x_n$) a vector with
18  * components $x_i$. A read valued function f of x, with the form
19  *
20  * f(x0, ..., xn-1) = c * x0^a1 * x2^a2 ... xn-1^an-1
21  *
22  * where $c$ > 0 and $a_i \in R$, is called a monomial function, or
23  * more informally, a monomial (of the variables $x_1$, ..., $x_n$).
24  * We refer to the constant $c$ as the coefficient of the monmial,
25  * and we refer to the constants $a_1$, ... $a_n$ as the exponents of
26  * the monomial. As an example, $2.3 x_1^2 x_2^{-0.15}$ is a monomial
27  * of the variables $x_1$ and $x_2$, with coefficient 2.3 and
28  * $x_2$-exponent -0.15.
29  * Any positive constant is a monomial, as is any variable. Monomials
30  * are closed under multiplication and division: if $f$ and $g$ are
31  * both monomials then so are $f*g$ and $f/g$. (This includes scaling
32  * by any positive constant.) A monomial raise to any power is also a
33  * monomial.
34  *
35  * The term `monomial', as used here (in the context of geometric
36  * programming) is similar to, but differs from the standard
37  * definition of `monomial' used in algebra. In algebra, a monomial
38  * has the form about, but the exponents $a_i$ must be non-negative
39  * integers, and the coefficent $c$ is one.
40  *
41  * @todo: In current implementation, the exponent _a is represented by
42  *only <double>. The type should be _Tp in general such that it can be also
43  *be a <AAF>.
44  */
