16 /**
17  * Posynomial function. A sum of one or more monomials, i.e., a
18  * function of the form
19  *
20  * f(x) = \sum_{k=1}^{K} c_k x_1^{a_{1k}} x_2^{a_{2k}} ... x_n^{a_{nk}},
21  *
22  * where $c_k$ > 0, is called a posynomial function or, more simply, a
23  * posynomial (with $K$ terms, in the variables $x_1$, ..., $x_n$. The
24  * term `posynomial' is meant to suggest a combination of `positive'
25  * and `polynomial'.
26  *
27  * Any monomial is also a posynomial. Posynomials are close under
28  * addition, multiplication, and positive scaling. Posynomials can be
29  * divided by monomials (with the result also a posynomial): If $f$ is
30  * a posynomial and $g$ is a monomial, then $f/g$ is a posynomial.
31  * Note that <_Tp> could be <double> or <AAF>
32  */
